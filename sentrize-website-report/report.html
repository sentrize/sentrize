The blunt verdict

Two hard problems make it read as "not a real company"

Everything else — the clashing colors, the thin type, the three taglines — reinforces the impression. But no amount of visual polish fixes these two. Fix them first.

01 · It's a cloned template

The entire front-end is a lifted Verifone.com (payments-terminal company) theme — Verifone's font, stylesheets, payment-terminal icons, even a leftover audio widget. A firm selling custom software shipped a cloned website. Any engineer sees it in DevTools in 10 seconds.

02 · The proof is fabricated

Stock-photo "team," testimonial authors whose names change between pages, your CEO also quoted as a customer, borrowed Cisco/Bitdefender logos with no matching case study, and a movie-reserved fake phone number. 90 seconds of checking exposes all of it.

§3

Do it in this order

⚖ Legal note

Displaying client logos and SOC 2 / ISO 27001 / HIPAA claims you can't substantiate — and a Sydney HQ with no ABN — is trademark & false-advertising exposure (incl. Australian Consumer Law). The P0 items below are risk, not just optics.

P0Trust & legalthis week
  • Remove all third-party client logos until each has permission + a matching named case study.
  • Delete fabricated testimonials and the stock-photo team page. Ship real people or none.
  • Remove unverifiable certs & awards until you hold them and can link the verification.
  • Replace the fake phone number; add your registered entity + ABN/ACN to the footer.
  • Cut the stats to only what you can source and defend.
P1De-Verifone the buildremoves the "cloned" tell
  • Grep to zero: verifone · sh__ · chrome.css · enterprise.css · en0000000000000812 · theme-teal · beyond-payments · blink-icon · colorbox · volume-booster
  • Self-host a licensed font; delete all Verifone-Sans references.
  • Swap the payment-terminal icons for one consistent software/cloud icon set.
P2Brand & design systemthe "looks generated" polish
  • Pick one tagline — use it verbatim in title, og:title, twitter:title, and h1.
  • Pick one accent color as a CSS variable; collapse backgrounds to white + one neutral + one dark.
  • Heading weights 600–700, body 400; whole-pixel type scale; darken muted text to pass WCAG AA.
  • Three-value radius scale (10 / 16 / 999px); replace stock imagery with real work.
P3Copy, IA & technicalfinish the job
  • Rewrite copy with specifics — kill "with confidence," "partner not a vendor," and the CTA duplicated on 4 pages.
  • Cut nav to ~5 items; fix the Pricing menu whose 5 links all go to one page.
  • Serve over HTTPS, redirect apex→canonical, add HSTS & JSON-LD.
  • Fix accessibility: real <main>, a working mobile menu, heading structure, unique link text, focus rings, lazy images.

A smaller, truthful site — one positioning line, 2–3 real people, one or two genuinely named case studies, one accent color — will out-convert this every time. The offering isn't the problem; the borrowed shell and invented proof are.
